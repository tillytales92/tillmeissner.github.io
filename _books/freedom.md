A sweeping portrait of an American family — the Berglunds of St. Paul, Minnesota — over several decades, *Freedom* is a novel about exactly what its title suggests: the impossible, contradictory things people mean by freedom, and the damage they do to each other and themselves in pursuit of it. Walter wants to save the cerulean warbler. Patty wants to escape her mother. Joey wants money and to escape his parents. Richard wants to remain pure. Nobody gets what they want in the way they wanted it.

Franzen writes social realism with an almost uncomfortable accuracy — the novel captures the texture of American middle-class life, its political anxieties, its environmental guilt, its sexual restlessness, with a precision that feels both clinical and empathetic. The Patty sections, written in the second person as an autobiography, are among the finest extended pieces of character writing in recent American fiction.

Dense, funny, and genuinely ambitious. A novel that takes the novel form seriously.
