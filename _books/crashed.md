The definitive account of the 2008 financial crisis and its long political aftershocks. Tooze dismantles the comfortable narrative that the crisis was a contained Anglo-American banking failure quickly resolved by technocratic intervention. Instead, he reveals it as a transatlantic rupture with profound and lasting consequences: the Eurozone debt crisis, the rise of austerity politics, the weakening of centrist democracy, and ultimately the conditions that produced Brexit and Trump.

What sets *Crashed* apart is its scope and its refusal to separate finance from geopolitics. Tooze traces the crisis through the Federal Reserve's dollar swap lines, the IMF's conditionality politics in Eastern Europe, and the ECB's belated interventions with equal command. The result is a book that is simultaneously a work of economic history, political analysis, and a warning about the fragility of the global order.

Demanding but richly rewarding — one of the most important books written about the political economy of the 21st century.
