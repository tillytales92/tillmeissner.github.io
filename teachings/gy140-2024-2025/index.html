Introduction to Geographical Research (GY 140)

Graduate Teaching Assistant for the Applied Quantitative Methods module (Winter Term 2024/25). Delivered 4 groups of lab seminars covering core statistical concepts and methods using STATA, R, and Excel.

Course: GY 140 – Introduction to Geographical Research     Term: Winter Term 2024/25     Role: Graduate Teaching Assistant (Quantitative Methods Module, Weeks 7–11)

This course introduces students to the production of geographical and environmental knowledge, covering both quantitative and qualitative research methods. I was responsible for the Applied Quantitative Methods module in the Winter Term (Weeks 7–11), teaching 4 seminar groups in computer lab sessions alongside lectures by Dr Filippo Boeri. Students applied statistical methods using STATA SE-18, R, and Excel.

Course Outline Class Instructions

Course Structure (Quantitative Methods Module)

Winter Term – Weeks 7–11 (March–April 2025)

Week Dates Topic
7 3–7 Mar Introduction to Quantitative Data (lecture only, no class)
8 10–14 Mar Centre, Spread, Populations and Samples
9 17–21 Mar Randomness and the Normal Distribution
10 24–28 Mar Interval Estimates and Hypothesis Testing
11 31 Mar–4 Apr Relationship between Variables + Recap Session

Each class was a 2-hour computer lab seminar. In Week 11, two sessions were held per group to allow for a full recap before the summative assessment.

Software

Students were trained in three statistical packages:

  • STATA SE-18 — inferential statistics and econometric analysis
  • R & RStudio — open-source statistical computing
  • Excel — exploratory data analysis and visualisation

Component Weight Deadline
