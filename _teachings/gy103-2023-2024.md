**Course:** GY 103 – Contemporary Europe &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Term:** Autumn Term 2023/24 &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Role:** Graduate Teaching Assistant

This course covers the economics and politics of European integration. As class teacher, I was responsible for 4 seminar groups (approx. 80 students) running alongside the main lecture series, delivered by Prof. Riccardo Crescenzi. Classes consolidated lecture content through student-led debates, reading card exercises, and regional policy pitches.

## Course Structure

**Block A – The Political Economy of European Integration (Weeks 2–11)**

| Week | Topic | Activity |
|------|-------|----------|
| 2 | History, Facts, Law & Institutions of European Integration | Intro class |
| 3 | Economics of Preferential Liberalisation & Market Size Effects | Reading card 1 |
| 4 | Growth Effects and Factor Market Integration | Reading card 2 · **Debate 1** |
| 5 | Location Effects, Economic Geography and Regional Policy | Reading card 3 · **Debate 2** |
| 6 | *Reading week* | — |
| 7 | The EU Budget | Essay 1 due |
| 8 | EU Cohesion Policy: Regional Policy (10.2–10.7) | Reading card 4 |
| 9 | EU Cohesion Policy: Evidence & Impact | Reading card 5 · **Policy Pitch** |
| 10 | The Common Agricultural Policy | Reading card 6 · **Policy Pitch** |

---

## Debate Topics

- **Debate 1:** Has economic integration improved competitiveness and boosted economic growth in the European Union?
- **Debate 2:** Has economic integration been a driver of increased regional inequality in the European Union?
